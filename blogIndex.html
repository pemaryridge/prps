The corridors of Pemary Ridge Primary School are buzzing with excitement as the newly established Mathematics Club opens its doors to eager learners. Launched this term, the club has quickly become a hub for students who share a passion for numbers, problem-solving, and mathematical exploration.

The Mathematics Club was created in response to a growing interest among students for a platform where they could engage with mathematics beyond the classroom. Mr. DR Sewsunker the school principal, explains, “The aim is to make mathematics more accessible and enjoyable for students. We want them to see math not just as a subject, but as a tool for understanding the world.”


The club meets weekly during break. During these sessions, students work on a variety of activities ranging from mathematics games, solving challenging puzzles and discussing famous mathematical problems, to preparing for math competitions and Olympiads. The club’s inclusive environment ensures that all students, regardless of their mathematical ability, feel welcome. The focus is on growth and learning at your own pace.

Looking ahead, the club has ambitious plans. They hope to organize inter-school math competitions, participate in national and international Olympiads, and perhaps even host a school-wide Mathematics Day. There are also discussions about creating a student-led math tutoring program to help peers struggling with the subject. “We’re just getting started, but the enthusiasm from the students is incredible,” says Mr. DR Sewsunker. “I’m confident this club will not only improve our students’ mathematical abilities but also foster a deeper appreciation for the subject.”

As the Mathematics Club continues to grow, it promises to be a cornerstone of Pemary Ridge Primary School’s extracurricular offerings, inspiring students to explore the fascinating world of mathematics.
